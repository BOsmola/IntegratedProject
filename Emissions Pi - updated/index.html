    Project Title – Emissions Pi

    Project Description:

    • To use a custom-built device based on a Raspberry Pi and SDS011 sensor to monitor emissions in Glasgow city centre and compare our readings to readings captured by Glasgow city council in 2019. By comparing these readings, we can analyse whether the covid pandemic had an effect on the rate of emissions in the city centre.
    • We achieved this by first calibrating our device at the Rutherglen air monitoring station, following this we set up our device at the Townhead air monitoring station on three separate occasions and took readings for 30 minutes at a time.
    • 1st set of readings captured on 30/03/2022 9AM, 12PM & 5PM 
    • 2nd set of readings captured on 31/03/2022 9AM, 12PM & 5PM 
    • 3rd set of readings captured on 01/04/2022 12PM & 5PM
    • We could only take readings when the humidity was below 70% as readings taken beyond this level are unreliable. This led to us being unable to take a reading on the morning of 01/04/2022 as the humidity was 89%.
    • Once We had taken our readings, we used a piece of 3rd party software called data wrapper to visualise our data.
    • However after reviewing our data we decided it was too unreliable to use for our comparison, this was one of the risks we foreseen in our risk assessment. We instead used publicly available data to make our comparison to ensure we had the most accurate information possible.
