  • For our group project, we have chosen to capture emissions data in Glasgow City Centre and compare this data with the levels taken by Glasgow City Council from before March 2020.
  • This is in order to highlight and analyze the impact that the pandemic has had on peoples travel habits and determine whether our environmental harm has diminished in that timeframe.
  • We believed that less people would be travelling due to working from home measures, as well as people being more likely to take public transport if they did have to travel and sought to investigate if our assumptions were true.

  • To do this we have gained access to the data that was already available from the council and set up our own Raspberry Pi to capture our own data at similar points throughout the city to ensure a fair comparison.
  • We have captured data from three points, at peak times during the day where traffic should be at its highest.
  • We have created charts that clearly depict the differences between both data sets, enabling us to draw conclusions based on the contrasts between the two.
  • The project has gone through several iterations along the way, which we will detail as we explain the planning phase.
